A little bit about me!

I was born and raised in NYC. I moved to Bucaramanga, Colombia when I was 12 to complete my middle and high school years. I graduated from high school in 2018 with an emphasis in science and mathematics, with a determination to use engineering as a means to be the change I wanted to see in my community. As such, I moved back to NYC to pursue my undergraduate career; and majored in Mechatronic Engineering due to its multidisciplinary engineering application by combining mechanical and electrical engineering, along with coding. This interdisciplinary field allowed me to be part of the full life-cycle of projects, which I immediately fell in love with! And since 2019, I've been involved in numerous teams to invent solutions that better my community's living, build competitive robots and rovers, and design hardware for flagship and research and development areas!

Giving back to my community

I recognize that who I am today is a reflection of the people and community who helped me grow and shaped my journey. Because of them, I carry a deep connection to my culture and identity, and I feel a strong push to give back in any way I can, which fuels a great deal of my projects and passions. From participating in profesional organizations to volunteering, I love to figure out how I can support and encourage those around me to reach their goals or learn something new along the way!

Society of Women Engineers member

I joined my college's SWE chapter during my first year at Vaughn College in the fall of 2019. During my undergrad, I: lead and participated in over 15 social outreach STEM workshops to involve elementary, middle, and high school students in the field; conducted professional and networking events for the chapter's members, including alumni dinner night and industry tours; and as the chapter's secretary and vice-president during the 2020-2021 and the 2021-2022 school year, respectively, coordinated the logistics for the chapter's annual conference attendance.

Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers member

I also joined my college's SHPE chapter during my first year at Vaughn College - my focus was always about finding and creating a supportive community around me! During my undergrad, I: conducted professional and networking events for the chapter's members to prepare members for upcoming conferences and took part in conference challenges such as the Extreme Engineering Challenge. Fun fact: I received my full-time offer at JPL from the 2022 National Convention!

Latin American and Caribbean Consortium of Engineering Institutions (LACCEI) participant

LACCEI is a non-profit organization consisting of institutions that offer academic programs in Engineering and Technology with the goal of collaborating with other LACCEI partners, including universities, colleges, and schools. I found out about this organization through the Engineering department's chair. LACCEI holds a yearly student competition where university students from around the world can showcase innovative designs and proposals that can be implemented in South America. Thanks to its community-centered vision, I proposed and worked on community-based research projects that aimed to improve the livelihood of my latino community in Colombia, given some social issues I observed growing up. More on these projects can be found here!

Brooke Owens Fellowship Finalist, Class of 2023

The Brooke Owens Fellowship (BOF) was established to help address a historic gender imbalance in the aerospace field. Brooke Owens Fellows are selected for their talent, their experience to date, their commitment to service, and their creativity. I applied to be part of the BOF class of 2023 and was accepted as a finalist! Although I did not move forward with the fellowship to pursue my full-time employment at JPL, I am forever grateful for the opportunity to be recognized among a pool of such talented people! Check out my acceptance reaction by none other than THE Emily Calandrelli!

Aviation Week's 20 Twenties, Class of 2022

Photo Credit: Chris Zimmer Photography

This program is a workforce initiative to inspire the next generation of aerospace and defense professionals. The award recognizes the top 20 aerospace-bound students in their twenties who are on the course to change the face of the aerospace and defense industry. This program not only brings fellow space-enthusiastics together, but it also connects students with industry leaders worldwide. Fun fact, I was one of of the two first Vaughn College students to receive this national distinction! While at the Luncheon in Washington DC, I got the opportunity to get interviewed on what the award and being part of the program meant to me, check it out here!

AIAA ASCEND Diversity Scholar, Class of 2022

This program seeks to provide opportunities for underrepresented students interested in aerospace to attend the ASCEND conference, with all expenses covered, in Las Vegas, Nevada. I met some of the brightest minds in the aerospace industry, ranging from astronauts, directors, engineers, and scientists to fellow scholars! It's an amazing opportunity to find out about up and coming space-related topics such as orbital safety policies, national security space, space rescue services, space debris, sustainability, and the global cooperation to accelerate space exploration.
