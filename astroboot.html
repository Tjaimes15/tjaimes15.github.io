What's this testing about? 🌕

Summary:

1. Project funded for the FY25 by the NASA Engineering and Safety Center
2. Test provides the Exploration Extra-vehicular Mobility Unit (xEMU) government reference design performance data and Exploration Extravehicular Activity Services (xEVAS) vendor risk reduction
3. The goal of this test was to provide additional insight to the xEMU Lunar Boot's performance and thermal model

Responsabilities:

• Designed mechanical support equipment (MGSE) to optimize the thermal conductivity from the He to the stomp plate and enable low temperature readings, reaching ~50K
• Developed a detailed test procedure for the test startup, operations, and shutdown, along with system schematics to ensure safe, efficient, and repeatable tests under space-like conditions
• Structured the DAQ system to integrate additional Beckhoff modules for managing an extensive boot thermocouple arrangement, and programmed new terminals capable of accurately outputting temperatures under –200 °C
• Programmed a MATLAB script to analyze the boot’s time-progressive readings and automatically determine when steady state was reached

Results:

• Successful test campaign completion!
• The JSC xEMU team developed a thermal model based on the boot testing TC recordings •D etailed results will be shared in a future ICES paper
