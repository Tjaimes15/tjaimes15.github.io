What's this testing about? 🧊

Summary:

1. This project is part of the FY24 Topic Areas Research and Technology Development
2.The objective of this project was to develop a method of evaluating the scientific integrity of a collected water ice sample as compared to its parent material
3. Samples were collected in the CITADEL chamber using the ICECAP rotary sampling tool, pneumatically transfered into a glass vial
4.This study will help optimize sample tube design and in chamber monitoring for better visual confirmation of collected samples

Responsibilities:

• Led the testbed setup and robotic programming of a 3-DOF arm through the in-hose CASAH softwreinterfcae
• Designed mechanical ground support equipment to interface with Honeybee’s funnel while providing an efficient thermal path to the chamber backstop
• Implemented the mechanical and software integration of ICECAP onto a 3DOF robotic arm, facilitating sample acquisition from icy simulants and delivery to Honeybee’s funnel

Results:

• Successful test campaign completion!
• Collected ice samples were analyzed at the JPL Analytical Chemistry Lab using ion chromatography to measure the ionic species in the sample and was then compared to the pre-mixed water ice
• This demonstration can be adapted to mission relevant sampling tools and scientifically relevant ice dopants
