What's this project about? 🏫

Summary:

The BrailleBud is a low-cost teaching tool that encourages pre-K through first grade children to learn the Braille alphabet and acquire letter recognition skills through interactive methods that stimulate their tactile and auditory senses. The idea originated from the limited access to educational Braille resources due to high prices, and non-targeted functions in Latin America. Due to this, visually impaired and legally blind students have fewer materials to learn and review Braille, leading them to have a lower educational level. The objective of the project is to give back to the visually impaired community by providing an inexpensive Braille teaching tool that will help children and their families acquire the basic knowledge of Braille.

How it works:

• BrailleBud implements 5V solenoids whose pins act as braille dots, and these solenoids are placed side by side along three rows to simulate a Braille cell
• PIR sensors are utilized to keep track of the child’s placement on the device - if the child is hovering over the braille cell, the speaker plays an audio congratulating them, and the program goes onto the following case. Otherwise, an audio encouraging the child to try again is played, and the program loops the case until it detects the child’s movement over the cells
• The design lives in a compact housing to enable learning on the go

Idea:

Design and develop a learning tablet aimed to help encourage children, aged two to five, to learn while playing and develop well pre-literacy skills to go onto literacy. The BrailleBud is programmed with Arduino and includes passive infrared sensors (PIR), a DFPlayer, and 5-volt solenoids.

Responsibilities:

• Simulated electrical design in Tinkercad to validate and debug Arduino code, ensuring smooth integration with hardware • Programmed 5V solenoids with PIR sensors to detect the movement of a child’s hand above the tablet
• Assembled a compact circuit design to include the device’s electrical components, including the PIR sensors and solenoids
• Managed the team to deliver a functional proof of concept, from proposal to implementation within 6 months, and later presenting the work to international representatives for broader adoption and community implementation

Results:

• BrailleBud can make a big difference when choosing a method to learn to read braille from an early age due to its convenience of price, the relatively simple construction of the device, and the interaction in the learning process
